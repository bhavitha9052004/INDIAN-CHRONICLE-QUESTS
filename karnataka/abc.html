Belur & Halebidu

Masterpieces of Hoysala architecture

Historical Significance

Belur and Halebidu, located in the Hassan district of Karnataka, India, are renowned for their exquisite Hoysala architecture. Belur was the early capital of the Hoysala Empire, and Halebidu was its later capital. The temples in these towns were built during the 12th century CE under the patronage of the Hoysala rulers.

Cultural Significance

The temples of Belur and Halebidu are celebrated not only for their architectural brilliance but also for their cultural significance. They serve as important religious centers and showcase the finest examples of Hoysala art and craftsmanship. These temples attract visitors from around the world who marvel at their intricate carvings and sculptures.

Architecture

The temples of Belur and Halebidu exhibit a unique style of architecture characterized by intricate carvings, sculptures, and detailed ornamentation. The Chennakesava Temple in Belur and the Hoysaleswara Temple in Halebidu are prime examples of this architectural style, featuring ornate pillars, sculptures of deities, and intricately carved friezes.

Preservation Efforts

Efforts have been made to preserve and protect the temples of Belur and Halebidu. Conservation projects aim to maintain the structural integrity of these ancient monuments while ensuring that their cultural and historical significance is preserved for future generations to appreciate.
