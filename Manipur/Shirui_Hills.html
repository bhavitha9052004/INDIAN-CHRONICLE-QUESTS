Shirui Hills

A Floral Paradise Amidst Manipur's Majestic Peaks

Overview

Being one of the popular tourist attraction in Ukhrul, the Shirui Kashong Peak lies at an altitude of 2,835 m above sea level. Shirui Kashong Peak is famous for two things- the rivers flowing across Ukhrul and the crevices of the peak and the second one is Shirui lilies growing in this place. After the tireless climb-up one gets rewarded by a heavenly view of clouds that is mesmerising hovering around the dappled towns and villages, lush green and dense and peaceful forests. Other than this, one can even get a view of the rare birds nesting there which includes Blyth’s Tragopan, Mrs. Hume's bar-backed pheasant, and more. One thing that needs to be kept in mind is to carry trekking shoes and raincoat as visitors have tend to experience sudden showers when climbing the peak.

Flora and Fauna

Nestled amidst the picturesque Shirui Hill Range, Sirohi National Park, established in 1982, is renowned for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. This park is home to a variety of animals including tigers, leopards, and tragopans, as well as a huge array of bird species. Known for the stunning variety of flowers that bloom here, Sirohi National Park is a must-visit during the monsoons. Among the rare flora is the majestic Shirui lily(Lilium Mackliniae), the state flower of Manipur, which blooms from mid-May each year. Awarded by the Royal Horticultural Society in London, this pinkish-white flower is unique to the Shirui Hill Range. The name Shirui Lily originates from a British Botanist named F. Kingdom Ward who in 1948 discovered the uniqueness of this lily flower. The local name of the flower is Kashong Timrawon. According to local legends the Kashong Timrawon is also a protective spirit which resides on the Shirui Peak. Every year thousand of Scientists and Tourists all over the World come to see this precious flower. The ordinary looking Shirui lily blossoms on the hilltop during May – June. Visitors can also witness over 1,000 different flowering plants, including rare orchids and rhododendrons such as horamwon, shirungwon, khongwon, sirawon, phanwon, and tipkhawon. Additionally, the park shelters several endangered bird species, including Nongyin, Manipur’s state bird. The Shirui Hill Range’s splendid views, rare flora and fauna, and natural charm offer visitors an unforgettable experience in the lap of nature.

The Shirui Lily Festival

The Shirui Lily Festival, celebrated during the peak blooming season, honors Manipur's state flower, the rare and endangered Shirui Lily. This festival, held atop the Shirui hills, aims to spread awareness and conserve this unique flower, known for its pleasant fragrance. The festival provides an opportunity for people from various communities and tribes to come together, explore the highest hill-station of Manipur, the picturesque Ukhrul district, and gain deeper insights about the Tangkhul Naga tribe. Climbing the Shirui hills, one of the most interesting treks in Manipur, offers a chance to see the rare flower and is a treat for nature and adventure lovers. The five-day celebration is colorful, showcasing numerous traditional and cultural activities, including live music concerts, folk performances, traditional dances, indigenous games, art and handicraft exhibitions, ethnic food eateries, and adventure activities like camping and biking. Shirock, the musical extravaganza featuring live music concerts and competitions, is a major highlight, attracting crowds from across the state and neighboring regions, with performances by international rock bands like Nazareth and Extreme.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Shirui Hills is during the monsoon season when the Shirui Lily blooms, usually between May and June.

Location

Shirui Kashung Peak – 2835 m from the sea level. Most of the major rivers originate from the crevices and slopes of this Shirui Peak. Shirui is 18 km from Ukhrul Town and 97 km from Imphal.

Travelling to Shirui Hills

By Air

Nearest Airport is located at Imphal

By Train

Nearest Railway station is located at Dimapur of Nagaland

By Road

It is situated on the north-east direction about 18 Km from Ukhrul Town on the Ukhrul-Jessami road NH150.
