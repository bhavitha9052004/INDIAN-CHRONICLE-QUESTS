About Sangai Festival

The Sangai Festival is a grand extravaganza celebrated every year from 21-30 November in the extremely beautiful region of Sangai in Manipur. The region is named so due to the rare species of deer that can be found only here. The Manipur Tourism Department makes sure that this eloquent celebration does not fall short on any terms as the primary goal of this festival is to promote Manipur as a promising upcoming tourist attraction. The festival is celebrated all across the state and the primary locations comprise Mao, Ukhrul, Lamboikhongnangkhong, Uripok, KeibulLamjao, KhumanLampak, and HaptaKangjeibung.

Cultural significance of Sangai Festival

The main venue of the 10-day Sangai Festival is the HaptaKangjeibung and Bheigyachandra Open Air Theatre, known as the BOAT. The BOAT hosts various cultural programs, including traditional Manipuri dance and indigenous music, aiming to spread awareness and educate visitors about Manipur's rich cultural heritage. Additionally, the BOAT features specimen huts built by tribal locals, showcasing the unique architectural styles of different tribes. Sports events are organized at the KhumanLampak Sports Complex and Takmu Water Sports Complex, with local sports and boating at Loktak Lake, North-East India's largest freshwater lake, being major attractions. Tourists can enjoy the beauty of Loktak Lake, with its intriguing 'Phumsangs' or floating huts built on 'Phumdis,' adding to the charm. Nearby, KeibulLamjao National Park, the world's only floating national park, located on Loktak Lake, serves as the last natural home of the endangered Sangai, a brow-antlered deer found only in Manipur. The Sangai Festival, through its vibrant cultural performances and unique showcases, highlights the diverse cultural heritage and natural beauty of Manipur, making it a significant event for both locals and visitors.

More about Sangai Festival

In 2017, the Sangai Festival celebrated its 17th year, and the Tourism Department aims to continue this legacy, constantly striving to enhance this tourist haven each year. The 2017 fiesta saw participation from numerous countries, including Nepal, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Myanmar, Thailand, Indonesia, Japan, South Korea, Cambodia, Vietnam, Singapore, Maldives, Malaysia, Mauritius, and Zimbabwe. The Tourism Department has ensured comfortable accommodation options for all tourists. Hotels such as Hotel Imphal, Hotel Nirmala, The Classic Hotel, Hotel Bheigo, Hotel New Airlines, Hotel Anand Continental, Hotel Krishtina, Classic Grande, and Youth Hostel have been entrusted with the significant responsibility of handling the tourist influx.
