Nature's Hidden Gem

Overview

The Phawngpui Blue Mountain National Park or simply Phawngpui National Park is a gorgeous conservatory forest in Mizoram housing several rare animals and birds, adorned in tropical forests. The park overlooks rivers and mountain ranges further adding to its natural beauty and attractiveness. It also houses several species of birds and animals such as the Asiatic black bear and the black eagle. The pleasant climate allows tourists to truly enjoy the serenity of the park and what it has to offer. What gives this park a mandatory spot in your itinerary is that it is the ultimate hike for trekkers, hikers and adventure-seekers given it's fairly cast size. The incentive of the long hike is the scenic beauty of the park, also the opportunity to spot rare animals, birds and plants on the trail. The Blue Mountain makes for a picturesque, breathtaking view that one must see to believe.

About Phawngpui National Park

Phawngpui National Park also called Phawngpui Blue Mountain National Park was established in 1992, about 300 km from the closest city Aizawl, in the Saiha district towards the southeast of Mizoram. It is one of the two national parks of Mizoram. The area of the park is 50 kmÂ² making it big enough for trekking and other such adventurous activities.

Flora and Fauna

The park boasts a diverse array of flora and fauna, predominantly featuring subtropical broadleaf and tropical evergreen forests. The Phawngpui mountain peak, along with the surrounding reservatory forest, forms the core of the park's landscape. The area is enriched with grasses, orchids, oak trees, rhododendron trees, rare bamboo groves, medicinal basil, various blossoms, and the daphnia flower. The park is home to a significant variety of fauna, including numerous species of birds, land animals, and reptiles. Among the animals found here are leopards, tigers, mountain goats, Asiatic black bears, barking deer, gorals, serows, slow lorises, and common langurs. Bird species include falcons, sunbirds, mountain bamboo partridges, Blyth's tragopans, Oriental honey buzzards, woodpeckers, common flamebacks, large-billed crows, and purple cocas. Various eagle species, such as the black eagle, mountain hawk eagle, sparrow hawk, tawny eagle, greater spotted eagle, and short-toed snake eagle, can also be observed. Additionally, the park is home to several species of reptiles, amphibians, and fish.

The unique name of the park comes from the highest mountain peak in the state - The Phawngpui Peak at the height of 2157 m. 'Phawngpui' translates to 'grassland' in English; also the mountain is covered with a thin layer of clouds and appear blue from a distance; thus it's the appropriate name.

Places to Visit

Phawngpui Peak

The highest point in the state, Phawngpui Peak is a revered mountain among the tribes of the state. The beautiful blue mountain located some 300 km south of Aizawl is a wonderful place to be and to bring out the adventure enthusiast in you. The peak is located within the Phawngpui National Park and oversees a wondrous landscape full of deep crevices and magnificent cliffs. Trekking to the summit of Phawngpui is an experience no adventure lover can let go of. The highest mountain in the state at 2100 meters, Phawngpui Peak is considered to be the abode of the goddess of the Mizo tribe. The peak is located within the Phawngpui National Park and oversees a wondrous landscape full of deep crevices and amazing cliffs.

Vantawng Waterfall

Vantawng Waterfall, the highest waterfall in the state, cascades from a height of 750 feet and is nestled amidst the lush green hills of Mizoram. Named after Vantawnga, an expert swimmer who could swim like a fish and reportedly performed stunts near the waterfall, it is a spectacular sight surrounded by dense bamboo groves and tropical vegetation. The falls are located near Thenzawl, about 137 kilometers from Aizawl, the state capital, and can be best viewed from a distance due to the steep and rugged terrain. The surrounding area, rich in natural beauty and tranquility, makes it a popular spot for nature lovers and tourists seeking to experience the pristine wilderness of Mizoram.

Best Time to Visit

The park is located at an elevation of about 7000 feet making the climate enjoyable all year round. The mountain ranges and rivers further accentuate the pleasantness of the park. The average rainfall is 3000 mm. The recommended time for visitation is between November and April.

Location

The Phawngpui Blue Mountain National Park is located in the district of Saiha towards the southeast of Mizoram. It overlooks the Chhimtuipui river and the hill ranges of Myanmar. The Kolodyne river is also in its vicinity, towards the east. The highest point in Mizoram which is the Phawngpui Peak (2157 m) is located inside the park
