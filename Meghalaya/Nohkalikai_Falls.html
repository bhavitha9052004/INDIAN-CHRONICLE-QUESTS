About Nohkalikai Falls

The fourth highest waterfalls in the world, Nohkalikai falls plunge some 335 meters from a verdant cliff to the ground giving the portrayal of immense and natural magnificence. The pride of the state of Meghalaya, the falls are one of the most beautiful and grand waterfalls in the country. Tucked in between the evergreen rainforest of the Khasi Hill and flowing with all its majesty and might, the falls plunges into a lagoon that is as blue as the afternoon sky.

History of Nohkalikai Falls

Nohkalikai Falls have been named so after a tragic Khasi legend. According to the legend, a woman named Likai had to remarry after her husband, who was a porter by profession, died on a trip carrying iron to Sylhet. Ka Likai (Ka is the prefix given for the female gender in Khasi) was left with her infant girl with no means of income so she had to become a porter herself. Her work required her to leave her daughter unattended for long intervals but when she would be at home she would spend most of her time taking care of her infant. Ka Likai, who married a second time, couldn’t pay attention to her second husband. The jealous husband killed the infant and cooked her meat after throwing away her head and bones. When Ka Likai returned home, she saw nobody in the house but a meal was ready. She wanted to go look for her daughter but she ate the meat as she was tired from work. Ka Likai usually had a betel leaf after her meals but she found a severed finger near the place where she usually cut betel nuts and betel leaves. Ka Likai realized what had happened in her absence and went mad with anger and started running as she swung a hatchet in her hand. She ran off the edge of the plateau and the waterfall where she jumped from was named Nohkalikai Falls after her. ‘Noh’ means to jump in Khasi.

Local Attractions

Nohkalikai Falls is surrounded by several local attractions that offer unique experiences for visitors. One of the most popular attractions is the Eco Park, which provides stunning views of the falls and the surrounding landscape. The park also offers a range of activities such as zip-lining, trekking, and bird watching. Another must-visit attraction is the Seven Sisters Falls, also known as the Nohsngithiang Falls, which is located nearby and is one of the tallest waterfalls in India. For those interested in local culture and heritage, a visit to the Khoh Ramhah rock formation is recommended. This giant rock resembles a basket or cone and offers panoramic views of the valley below. Additionally, the Thangkharang Park, which is located about 12 kilometers from the falls, offers a garden, playground, watchtower, and viewpoint, providing stunning views of the waterfall and the valley.

Location

Nohkalikai Falls is situated near Cherrapunji in the East Khasi Hills district of Meghalaya, India, about 7 kilometers from the main town. Accessible by a short drive from Cherrapunji, visitors can reach the falls via well-maintained roads. The journey offers scenic views of the lush, verdant landscape. Public transportation options, such as buses and shared taxis, are available from Shillong to Cherrapunji, followed by a local taxi ride to the falls. The best time to visit is during the monsoon season, when the falls are most impressive.

Each of these facilities ensures a memorable stay, enhancing the experience of visiting Nohkalikai Falls.

Local Foods and Restaurants

At Nohkalikai Falls, visitors can savor a variety of local Khasi dishes that showcase the region's culinary traditions. Delightful offerings include Jadoh, a fragrant rice and meat dish cooked with spices; Pumaloi, a steamed rice cake wrapped in turmeric leaves; and Doh Khlieh, a zesty salad of minced pork, onions, and chilies. For a richer flavor experience, try Doh Neiiong, a creamy pork curry with black sesame seeds. Another must-try is pork cooked with bamboo shoots, offering a tangy twist. To complement these, Tungrymbai, a spicy fermented soybean dish, adds depth to the local culinary adventure.

In the vicinity of Nohkalikai Falls, several local eateries offer authentic Khasi cuisine and delightful dining experiences. Ri Kynjai Restaurant provides a taste of local flavors in a serene setting, while Cafe Cherra offers a variety of dishes with a view. The Cliffside Restaurant provides a unique dining experience overlooking the valley, and Cloud 9 Restaurant offers a range of local and international dishes in a cozy atmosphere. These restaurants cater to different tastes, providing a culinary adventure for visitors to Nohkalikai Falls.

Activities

Trekking:

Nohkalikai Falls offers trekking opportunities like the Nongriat Trek to the Double Decker Living Root Bridge, a challenging but rewarding journey through scenic landscapes. The Nohsngithiang Trek to the Seven Sisters Falls is moderate and starts from Eco Park, offering views of diverse flora and fauna. The Mawmluh Trek is an easy but adventurous trail to the Mawsmai Cave, known for its limestone formations.

Camping:

Eco Park and Halari Viewpoint offer camping facilities with basic amenities amidst nature. Tyrna Village provides a cultural experience with homestays and camps, allowing visitors to experience the local lifestyle.

Sightseeing:

Nohkalikai Viewpoint offers the best views of the waterfall and the pool, while Thangkharang Park provides a garden, playground, watchtower, and viewpoint. Khoh Ramhah offers unique views of the waterfall and the valley.

Photography:

Nohkalikai Falls provides various angles and perspectives for photographers, with sunrise, sunset, and cloudy days offering the best lighting conditions. Each moment captures a different aspect of the falls, from the golden hues of sunrise to the dramatic shadows of sunset and the mysterious atmosphere of cloudy days.

Travelling

  • Air:Nearest airport for domestic flights from the north-east states is at Umroi which is 35 km from Shillong whereas for flights from other major cities Guwahati is the nearest airport which 133 km from Cherrapunjee and 100 odd kilometers from Shillong.
  • Rail:Nearest rail head is at Guwahati. One can easily hire a cab or catch a bus till Shillong and continue further from Shillong till Cherrapunjee through bus or taxi.
  • Road:Cherrapunjee is 53 km from Shillong and is well connected by road. Many state and private buses ply from Bada Bazaar, Shillong till Cherrapunjee. From Cherrapunjee you can take a taxi or an auto till the Nohkalikai Falls.
