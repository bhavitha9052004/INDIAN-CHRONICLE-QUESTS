About Mawsynram

Mawsynram, situated in the East Khasi Hills district of Meghalaya, holds the distinction of being the wettest place on Earth. With its breathtaking landscapes and record-breaking rainfall, Mawsynram offers a unique destination for adventurous travelers. Located approximately 65 kilometers from Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, and 80 kilometers from Cherrapunji Mawsynram is a perfect destination for a day trip.

The 'Maw' in Mawsynram is a Khasi word meaning 'stone'. It symbolizes the unique megaliths unearthed in the Khasi Hill area. The village is well known for its huge formation of a stalagmite, which takes after the shape of a 'Shivling'. Situated 1400 meters above sea level, the rains are so powerful in Mawsynram that the local villagers have to make use of thick grass to sound-proof their homes from the thunderous rain.

Local Attractions

Mawlyngbna

Mawlyngbna, a village situated just a few picturesque kilometers south of Mawsynram, shares its hilltop with three others, offering mesmerizing views of the rolling Khasi hills blending into the plains of Bangladesh. This rain-blessed village, known for its warm and welcoming locals, has gained popularity among tourists for its diverse natural attractions.

Within Mawlyngbna, numerous natural springs provide clean, fresh water throughout the year, serving as both a vital resource and a refreshing spot for recreational activities. The Umkhakoi Lake, a serene waterbody is popular spot for river canyoning in Mawlyngbna. Surrounding the village is a sprawling broadleaf forest teeming with diverse flora and fauna, including the endangered predaceous pitcher plant, exclusive to this region.

For nature enthusiasts, following a trail north through the jungle canopy leads to a vast plateau of rock and grass, offering endless vistas under the open sky. This area is renowned for its geological significance, with embedded fossils putting Mawlyngbna on the map for earth science enthusiasts. Popular activities include exploring the mysterious Mawmluh Cave, swimming in the natural rock pools of Wei Sawdong, and embarking on guided treks to nearby attractions like the Um Diengkain Valley and Thum Falls. Venturing further west unveils a land steeped in folklore, where a winding rivulet leads to the legendary animal marketplace known as Ka Iew Luri-Lura, with rock impressions resembling animal footprints believed to be remnants of a time when animals could communicate. The rivulets also form cascades, including the notable Um Diengkain and Ar Phalat waterfalls, which serve as fertile breeding grounds for various fish species. To immerse visitors in local culture, the community hosts traditional dances and showcases handicrafts such as handlooms, baskets, and implements at the village museum. Additionally, ancient rest areas along village pathways offer glimpses into the area's rich heritage. The Mawlyngbna Autumn Festival, held annually in October, showcases traditional music, dance, and cuisine, providing a glimpse into Khasi culture.

Mawjymbuin Cave

Mawjymbuin Cave is situated in Mawjymbuin village in Mawsynram (the wettest place on earth) in Cherrapunji and is a religiously significant site. Made of calcareous sandstones and having numerous stalagmites (formed as a result of calcium carbonate depositions, weathering and dripping of mineral enriched liquid), the 209-metre high cave is of special interest to geologists who conduct special researches here. The Mawjymbuin Cave is also thronged by naturalists, photographers, pilgrims and tourists alike.

Mawjymbuin Cave, situated in Mawjymbuin village in Mawsynram (the wettest place on earth) in Cherrapunji, is a religiously significant site. Made of calcareous sandstones and featuring numerous stalagmites formed from calcium carbonate depositions and mineral-rich drips, this 209-meter high cave is of special interest to geologists who conduct extensive research here. The cave is also a popular destination for naturalists, photographers, pilgrims, and tourists alike. A primary attraction is a rock structure shaped like a shivalinga, which is highly revered by Hindus, alongside a thin stream flowing towards the eastern end of the cave. There are many rift entrances and passages, some of which are challenging to navigate. The Shivalinga in Mawjymbuin Cave holds immense cultural and spiritual importance, attracting devotees and curious explorers. The cave serves as a serene place for contemplation, with its natural ambiance enhancing the spiritual experience. The highlight of Mawjymbuin Cave is the colossal stalagmite that has formed into a Shivalinga, adding a sacred dimension to the site. This natural marvel draws visitors not only for its religious significance but also for the fascinating limestone formations, stalactites, and stalagmites that adorn the cave's interiors.

Krem Liat Prah Cave

Located in Mawsynram, Krem Liat Prah, also known as the "Cave of the Stone" in Khasi, stands as one of Meghalaya's most spectacular natural wonders. This expansive limestone cave system, renowned as one of the longest in the Indian subcontinent, offers an awe-inspiring experience for adventurous travelers. The highlight of Krem Liat Prah is its magnificent underground river, which winds its way through intricate passages and majestic chambers, adorned with stunning stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors to this geological marvel can expect to embark on a thrilling journey into the depths of the Earth, marveling at the ancient formations shaped over millennia. Popular activities at Krem Liat Prah include guided cave explorations, allowing visitors to delve into the subterranean world and witness the wonders hidden beneath the surface. While the cave is accessible year-round, it's advisable to visit during the dry season, typically from October to April, for optimal conditions.

Location

Mawsynram, the wettest place on earth, is located in the East Khasi Hills district of Meghalaya, India, at an elevation of about 1,400 meters above sea level. Accessible via a scenic two-hour drive from Shillong (65 km away), it is serviced by regular buses and shared taxis. The best time to visit is during the monsoon season (June to September), though roads can be challenging. Mawsynram offers lush landscapes and dramatic cloud formations year-round.

Accommodation

In Mawsynram, accommodation options blend comfort with the natural beauty of the region. Mawphlang Guest House offers cozy rooms and warm hospitality, providing a perfect retreat after exploring the wettest place on earth. Sohra Plaza combines modern amenities with local charm, making it a popular choice for travelers. Sa-i-Mika Park Resort offers cottages set amidst lush greenery, providing a tranquil stay with stunning views. Jiva Resort is renowned for its comfortable accommodations and serene ambiance, ensuring a relaxing experience. Each of these facilities offers a unique stay, enhancing the charm of Mawsynram.

Restaurants and Local Food

For the foodies, the eating choices in Mawsynram are limited although eating in Cherrapunjee can be a memorable experience. You can relish Khasi cuisine such as pork rice and more. Shops selling pork and other red meat flourish in the town. Sohra Pulao which is rice cooked with oil and vegetables without spices is a must try for everyone visiting this place. One will also find Indian Chinese, Punjabi and Bengali cuisines in Cherrapunjee. But the specialty of the region lies in the fact that theses will mostly be the Khasi version of these cuisines.

In Mawsynram, several top restaurants offer delightful culinary experiences amidst the lush landscapes. Orange Roots is a popular choice, known for its traditional Khasi cuisine and cozy ambiance. Sa-i-Mika Park offers a unique dining experience with a blend of local and continental dishes, set in a serene environment. Phidalia Kitchen stands out for its delicious home-cooked meals and friendly service. Cafe Cherrapunjee is favored for its picturesque setting and diverse menu, featuring both local and international fare. Each of these establishments provides a memorable dining experience, reflecting the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Mawsynram.
