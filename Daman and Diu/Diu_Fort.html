About Diu Fort

Diu Fort, located on the coast of Diu Island, is a remarkable example of Portuguese colonial architecture. Built in the 16th century, this fort stands as a testament to the strategic maritime prowess of the Portuguese. The fort offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and houses numerous cannons, a lighthouse, and several chapels. Its well-preserved structure and rich history make it a must-visit destination in Diu.

The fort is known for its expansive bastions, intricately carved gateways, and underground tunnels. Visitors can explore the fort's various sections, including the old barracks, powder magazines, and the remains of a once-thriving settlement. The fort also provides a serene atmosphere to enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding seascape.

Local Attractions

Diu Fort is surrounded by several attractions that offer a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Nagoa Beach

Nagoa Beach is a popular beach in Diu, known for its palm-fringed shorelines and crystal-clear waters. It is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

St. Thomas Church

St. Thomas Church, now a museum, is a beautiful example of Portuguese architecture. It houses an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits depicting the history of Diu.

Gangeshwar Temple

Gangeshwar Temple is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is situated by the sea, with five Shivalingas submerged in the water during high tide.

Naida Caves

Naida Caves are a network of underground tunnels and caves located near Diu Fort. These naturally formed caves are a fascinating site for exploration and photography.

INS Khukri Memorial

INS Khukri Memorial is a tribute to the naval ship and its brave crew who perished during the Indo-Pak war of 1971. The memorial is situated on a hill overlooking the sea, offering a tranquil spot for reflection.

Location

Diu Fort is located on the eastern end of Diu Island, making it easily accessible for visitors. The fort's coastal location offers breathtaking views and convenient access to other local attractions.

The best time to visit the fort is during the cooler months from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor exploration.

Reaching Diu Fort

Air

The nearest airport to Diu Fort is Diu Airport, which is well-connected to major cities like Mumbai. From the airport, you can take a taxi or local transportation to reach the fort.

Railways

Veraval Railway Station is the closest major railway station to Diu, located about 90 kilometers away. Regular train services connect Veraval to various cities, and from Veraval, you can reach Diu by taxi or bus.

Road

Diu Fort is easily accessible by road from nearby cities. You can drive from Ahmedabad, Rajkot, or Bhavnagar, or take a bus to Diu and then use local transportation to reach the fort.
